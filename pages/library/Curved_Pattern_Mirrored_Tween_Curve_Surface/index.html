Mirrored Tween Curve Surface

How It Works

The Mirrored Tween Curve Surface KiriInflate is generated by mirroring the Tween Curve Surface pattern, thus sharing similar geometric principles and deformation behavior. Its mirrored configuration enhances the overall bending capability, making it more effective in achieving pronounced curvature upon inflation.

Standard Sample Parameters

  • Material Thickness: 0.07 mm per side
  • Scale: 62.0 mm × 80.0 mm
  • Channel Width: 6.4 m
  • Slit Spacing: 4.8~5.4 mm

❗️Please note: In the design tool, the Channel Width is adjusted via the Uniform Channel Division bar. Besides, the Slit Spacing bar is used to adjust the Slit Spacing; however, the values do not correspond one-to-one, as they follow a non-linear curve.

Customization Options and Actuation Control

KiriInflate structures offer high programmability of geometric and mechanical properties through customization of slit patterns on 2D surfaces, allowing for rapid formation of stretchable 3D geometries with tailored mechanical behaviors. Key parameters to adjust include:

Scale

As the scale increases, the arch height increases significantly. Larger inflatables generally increase stretchable length and contraction range, while both contraction and tensile forces increase.

Key Structure Parameter: Channel Width

As the channel width increases, the arch height first decreases and then increases.

Material Thickness

As the thickness increases, the arch height increases significantly. What makes this behavior particularly tricky is that thicker actuators are more difficult to fully inflate, which paradoxically makes them more prone to folding and arching from the center.
