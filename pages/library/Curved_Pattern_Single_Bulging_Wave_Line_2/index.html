Bulging Wave Line 2

How It Works

The Bulging Wave Line 2 resembles a sine wave, but with broader peaks and troughs—its features appear more like half-ellipses, and the vertical segments are relatively steeper. This abrupt change in channel width results in a noticeable bending effect upon inflation. Unlike the lateral bending observed in the previously mentioned Nonuniform Channel – Asymmetric Side Slit, this deformation occurs perpendicular to the surface and is pronounced.

Standard Sample Parameters

  • Material Thickness: 0.07 mm per side
  • Scale: 15.50 mm × 180.00 mm
  • Channel Width at Narrowest Point: 3.4 mm

Customization Options and Actuation Control

KiriInflate structures offer high programmability of geometric and mechanical properties through customization of slit patterns on 2D surfaces, allowing for rapid formation of stretchable 3D geometries with tailored mechanical behaviors. Key parameters to adjust include:

Scale

As the scale increases, the curvature first increases and then decreases. Larger inflatables generally increase stretchable length and contraction range, while both contraction and tensile forces increase. However, increasing sizes may lead to a slight decrease in the contraction ratio due to material resistance. Here we assume that the material resistance plays a dominant role in the largest sample.

Key Structure Parameter: Number of Units & Width at the Narrowest Point

As the number of units increases, curvature remains almost unchanged initially, but decreases sharply as the number of units becomes too large. As the width at the narrowest point increases, the curvature first decreases and then increases.

Material Thickness

As the thickness increases, the curvature increases and and forms a more complete circle. In this particular case, increasing the thickness consistently helps to build up internal air pressure, resulting in stronger and more complete deformation of the structure.
