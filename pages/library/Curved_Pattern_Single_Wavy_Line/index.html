Single Wavy Line

How It Works

The Single Wavy Line is characterized by a high-curvature, snake-like undulating pattern. Upon inflation, this loosely arranged wave contracts and retracts, producing a tightening effect similar to that of crocheted yarn being pulled taut.

Standard Sample Parameters

  • Material Thickness: 0.07 mm per side
  • Scale: 63 mm × 197 mm
  • Number of Cycles: 5
  • Channel Width: 7.00 mm
  • Amplitude: 29.0 mm

❗️Please note: In the design tool, users need to jointly adjust the Channel Width parameter by controlling both the Primary Width Adjustment Parameter bar and the Secondary Width Adjustment Parameter bar.

Customization Options and Actuation Control

KiriInflate structures offer high programmability of geometric and mechanical properties through customization of slit patterns on 2D surfaces, allowing for rapid formation of stretchable 3D geometries with tailored mechanical behaviors. Key parameters to adjust include:

Scale

As the scale increases, the contraction ratio first increases until each cycle nearly completely overlaps, but then drops sharply when the scale continues to grow. Larger inflatables generally increase stretchable length and contraction range, while both contraction and tensile forces increase. However, Increasing sizes may see a slight decrease in contraction ratio due to material resistance. Here we assume that the material resistance plays a dominant role in the largest sample.

Key Structure Parameter: Amplitude & Number of Cycles

As the amplitude increases, the contraction ratio increases until each cycle nearly completely overlaps. As the number of cycles increases, the contraction ratio increases until each cycle nearly completely overlaps. Overall, this shape exhibits a good contraction ratio.

Material Thickness

As the cycle thickness increases, the contraction ratio initially rises but then drops sharply when the thickness becomes excessive. Theoretically, increasing material thickness reduces the deformation due to higher internal stress. However, it enhances both contraction and tensile forces.
