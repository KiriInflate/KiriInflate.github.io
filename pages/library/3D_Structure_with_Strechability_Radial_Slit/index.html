Radial Slit

How It Works

The Radial Slit KiriInflate features a radial slit pattern that emanates from the center, resembling a single column of Conventional Kirigami (as presented in Primitives) wrapped into a circular ring. This design enables a transformation from a 2D toroidal (donut-shaped) configuration to a 3D cylindrical shape upon inflation. This pattern grants the structure adaptability to cylindrical or spherical surfaces of varying diameters, making it suitable for integration with deformable biological tissues such as limbs, fingers, the abdomen, and even the heart.

Standard Sample Parameters

  • Material Thickness: 0.07 mm per side
  • Scale: 30.0 mm × 30.0 mm
  • Slit overlap distance: 4.8 mm
  • Outer Slit Length: 8.0 mm
  • Inner Slit Length: 8.0mm
  • Central Slit Length: 13.0mm
  • Outer Circle Radius: 30.0 mm
  • Inner Circle Radius: 10.0 mm
  • Number of Equal Divisions: 18

❗️Please note: In the design tool, Users can adjust the Slit Overlap Distance by modifying the Central Slit Position bar.

Customization Options and Actuation Control

KiriInflate structures offer high programmability of geometric and mechanical properties through customization of slit patterns on 2D surfaces, allowing for rapid formation of stretchable 3D geometries with tailored mechanical behaviors. Key parameters to adjust include:

Scale

As the scale increases, both the contraction ratio and the taper angle decrease. Larger inflatables generally increase stretchable length and contraction range, while both contraction and tensile forces increase. However, Increasing sizes may see a slight decrease in contraction ratio due to material resistance. Here we assume that the material resistance plays a dominant role.

Key Structure Parameter: Slit Overlap Distance

As the slit overlap distance increase, the contraction ratio first increases and then decreases, while the taper angle first decreases and then increases.

Material Thickness

As the thickness increases, the contraction ratio decreases progressively, while the taper angle remains nearly unchanged at first but drops sharply at a certain thickness.
