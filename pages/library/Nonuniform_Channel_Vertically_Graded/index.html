Nonuniform Channel – Vertically Graded

How it Works

The Nonuniform Channel – Vertically Graded KiriInflate is characterized by a graded variation in channel width along the vertical direction. Since the width of air channels significantly influences the stretchability of KiriInflate, introducing a gradient enables the actuator to exhibit spatially varying elasticity. During interaction, this allows the slits to open sequentially. Morphologically, the gradient also induces the airbag to curl toward one side.

Standard Sample Parameters

  • Material Thickness: 0.07 mm per side
  • Scale: 50 mm × 54 mm
  • Slip Overlap Distance: 15.0 mm
  • Channel Length: 40.0 mm
  • Starting Channel Width: 4.5 mm
  • Ending Channel Width: 9.0 mm
  • Number of Units: 4

Please note: In the design tool, the 'Slip Overlap Distance' is not a direct input. It is automatically calculated based on the 'Channel Length' and the 'Central Slits' length you define.

Customization Options and Actuation Control

KiriInflate structures offer high programmability of geometric and mechanical properties through customization of slit patterns on 2D surfaces, allowing for rapid formation of stretchable 3D geometries with tailored mechanical behaviors. Key parameters to adjust include:

Scale

Larger inflatables generally increase stretchable length and contraction range, while both contraction and tensile forces increase. Increasing sizes may see a slight decrease in contraction ratio due to material resistance.

Key Structure Parameter: Slit Overlap Distance

Increased overlap distance significantly enhances contraction ratio and stretchability, but concurrently decreases both contraction and tensile forces.

Material Thickness

Decreasing material thickness increases the contraction ratio due to lower internal stress. However, this also leads to lower contraction and tensile forces.
