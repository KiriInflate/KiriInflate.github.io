Bulging Wave Line 1

How It Works

The Bulging Wave Line 1 was inspired by lollipops, created by arranging multiple connected elliptical shapes with filleted joints at the connection points. Compared to version 2, it features smoother transitions at the wider sections of the channel. While both versions exhibit a similar bending effect upon inflation, the curvature in version 2 is more pronounced.

Standard Sample Parameters

  • Material Thickness: 0.07 mm per side
  • Scale: 25.0 mm × 158.0 mm
  • Channel Width at Narrowest Point: 11.0mm
  • Number of Units: 8

❗️Please note: In the design tool, the Channel Width at Narrowest Point bar controls the corresponding parameter, but its values do not map directly to actual width. Instead, the width increases proportionally based on a predefined scaling ratio.

Customization Options and Actuation Control

KiriInflate structures offer high programmability of geometric and mechanical properties through customization of slit patterns on 2D surfaces, allowing for rapid formation of stretchable 3D geometries with tailored mechanical behaviors. Key parameters to adjust include:

Scale

As the scale increases, the curvature first increases and then decreases. Larger inflatables generally increase stretchable length and contraction range, while both contraction and tensile forces increase. Here, the size of the units significantly influences the resulting curvature, which explains the observed trend of initially increasing and then decreasing curvature—a result that aligns with common intuition.

Key Structure Parameter: Number of Units & Width at the Narrowest Point

As the width at the narrowest point increases, the curvature decreases. As the number of units increases, the curvature changes only slightly; the circumference formed becomes larger.

Material Thickness

As the thickness increases, the curvature first increases and then decreases. Initially, increasing the material thickness enhances the internal pressure resistance of the structure, leading to greater deformation. However, beyond a certain threshold, the thickness begins to hinder deformation, resulting in reduced curvature.
