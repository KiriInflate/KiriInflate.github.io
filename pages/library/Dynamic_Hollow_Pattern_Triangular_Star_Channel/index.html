Triangular Star Channel

How It Works

The Triangular Star Channel KiriInflate draws inspiration from the Y-shaped slit design, forming a network of interconnected triangular star-shaped air channels. Upon inflation, each star contracts as the internal angles decrease, resulting in a dynamic, reconfigurable surface. This contraction reduces the size of the surface pores, enabling real-time modulation of porosity. Such a design holds promise for applications in tunable filtration, adaptive ventilation, and morphing architectural skins.

Standard Sample Parameters

  • Material Thickness: 0.07 mm per side
  • Scale: 114.5 mm × 101.0 mm
  • Channel Width: 5.4 mm
  • Horizontal Arrays Number: 3
  • Vertical Arrays Number: 3

❗️Please note: In the design tool, users can control the channel width by adjusting the Channel Width bar, which offsets the inner cut line based on the channel width of the standard sample.

Customization Options and Actuation Control

KiriInflate structures offer high programmability of geometric and mechanical properties through customization of slit patterns on 2D surfaces, allowing for rapid formation of stretchable 3D geometries with tailored mechanical behaviors. Key parameters to adjust include:

Scale

As the scale increases, the contraction of the Triangular Star decreases. Larger inflatables generally increase stretchable length and contraction range, while both contraction and tensile forces increase. However, Increasing sizes may see a slight decrease in contraction ratio due to material resistance. Here we assume that the material resistance plays a dominant role.

Key Structure Parameter: Channel Width & Array Number

As the channel width increases, the Triangular Star contracts more significantly. As the number of arrays increases , both horizontal and vertical, the Triangular Star contracts more significantly.

Material Thickness

As the thickness increases, the contraction of the Triangular Star decreases. Theoretically, increasing material thickness reduces the deformation due to higher internal stress. However, it enhances both contraction and tensile forces.
